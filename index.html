Hello, I an Tara Chen and I'm enrolling in an Art Major at UCR. I'm currently a freshman here, but I'm looking forward to expanding my knowledge for my art career and putting my talent to the test. The amount of work and passion put into my artworks will be beneficial to any future generations. Therefore when I graduate, I'm hoping to teach children about the experience of art. With AI going on, the meaning of why being an artist is not a real job anymore is bull. I want to remind people on why artists loves doing art and how it's better than what AI has to offer. I don't expect much in changing the future all that much, but I do want to have a decent job in giving passion for young artists and for me to enjoy myself.

My hobbies are art, no matter if it's traditional or digital I love quiet work enviornments I tend to put others before myself

My journeys on being an art major starts off when I was around 6. My mother placed me in a drawing class where I drew animals in some enviornment. That moment got me really into drawing animals and so I continued on my journey. In elementary School, I attended some yearbook cover contest and I've got praised for my drawing and won 2nd place in that contest. That praise allow me to continue drawing, it was peaceful and I love looking at all the details and painting them. Then in Middle School, I took it to the next level and started doing realism as I shade tomatos and my own shoes onto paper to make it come to life. I enjoy looking at my masterpiece, but I still hanted to challenge myself. Soon in High School, I started to draw humans so I can practice drawing anatomies. That's the phase that I'm currently at and thus it leads me to today.
